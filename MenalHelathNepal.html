Despite growing awareness, mental health issues are still under-treated, and stigma remains a barrier. Globally, one in eight people live with a mental health condition, ranging from depression and anxiety to more severe illnesses such as schizophrenia. The COVID-19 pandemic exacerbated these challenges, leading to rising cases of anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Despite increased discussions about mental health, funding gaps and lack of comprehensive care remain persistent issues. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), mental health conditions contribute to 16% of the global burden of disease among people aged 10 to 19, highlighting the vulnerability of younger populations. In low-income countries, the mental health system infrastructure is limited, with most mental health services concentrated in urban areas, leaving rural populations with little to no access to necessary care. WHO continues to urge countries to invest in mental health services and integrate mental health into primary healthcare systems.
